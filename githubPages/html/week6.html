Use Case Diagram

We created a use case diagram to visually represent the interactions between the users and the Freight Shield System. This diagram serves as a tool to communicate the various functionalities available to different users within the system. The 'Admin' role is responsible for checking both shipper and carrier compliance, while the 'Shipper' has the ability to post a load, which then appears on the shipper dashboard. The 'Carrier Dashboard' enables the 'Dispatcher' to pick a load and assign a driver, and it also extends to features such as live tracking and logbook integration. The 'Driver' interacts with the system via the mobile app, utilizing it for live updates. These interactions are streamlined and interconnected, ensuring that each user has the necessary tools and information to perform their roles effectively within the logistics and shipment tracking process.

Sequence Diagram

We have created a sequence diagram to show the order of actions within the Freight Shield System, mapping out the communication between different components and stakeholders. The process begins with the 'Shipper' posting a load, which is then sent to the 'Shipper Dashboard' and subsequently displayed on the 'Loadboard'. As the sequence progresses, the 'Logbook' receives and sends data, playing a pivotal role in maintaining records. 'Live Tracking' is an integral feature that enables the sending and receiving of real-time location updates to and from the 'Mobile App', ensuring the 'Driver' is continually updated. On the receiving end, the 'Carrier Dashboard' is where the 'Dispatcher' interacts with the system, visualizing the load and then assigning it to the 'Driver'. Notifications and data exchange between the 'Shipper', 'Driver', and 'Dispatcher' facilitate a cohesive operational environment, showcasing the system's ability to synchronize logistics and communication effectively.
